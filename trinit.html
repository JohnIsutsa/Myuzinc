SYLLABUS
The Trinit Music syllabus focuses on cultivating and developing our pupil’s skills and knowledge through providing them direct experience in the components of Music reading, Performance, Interpretation, Ear work, Composition, improvisation and recording. While these components are interconnected the learning outcomes at each level delineate the progressive development in each component would contribute to a holistic music experience. It’s based and fined on liberal arts curriculum and covers both practical and theory. It offers diverse expressions and opportunities that define music of today so that our students can easily employ their musical education in a global society, with a taste of heritage and cultural authenticity. Pupils are engaged in active listening as opposed to passive hearing of music, that way appreciation and understanding of creative arts is established and built upon. Through these activities, they are guided to cultivate a sensitive musical ear. Opportunities abound for pupils to perform in showcases, ensemble settings, creative use of music aesthetics and competitions so that experience in collaborative music making would enhance their abilities in listening out for accuracy, blending and balance.
